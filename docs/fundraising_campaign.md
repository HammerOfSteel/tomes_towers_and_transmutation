## The Pitch — One Paragraph

*Tomes, Towers & Transmutation* is a browser-native isometric action-RPG where you are not the hero saving the princess. You are the princess. You've taught yourself the wizard's own magic, you've freed yourself from his tower, and now you're going to recruit a monster army and ruin his day. It's *A Link to the Past* meets *Hades* meets a very specific kind of revenge comedy. Four playable species, distinct talent trees, a tower full of lore written in someone else's margin notes, and a power curve that ends somewhere between "probably illegal" and "personally satisfying." Currently in late alpha. Your support ships the full build.

**Long description (Steam format):**
```
ABOUT THIS GAME

Tomes, Towers & Transmutation subverts the oldest trope in fantasy: the princess locked in a tower.

You didn't ask to be here. The wizard didn't ask if that was okay. He also left his entire library of arcane texts in your cell, which suggests a certain lack of threat assessment on his part.

You have taught yourself everything. You have broken out. You have claimed the tower.

Now comes the interesting part.

FOUR SPECIES, FOUR DESTINIES
Choose who you are at the campfire — human, undead, vulperia (fox-folk), or slime. The wizard will ask you several diagnostic questions. Your answers determine your starting stats. His questions are revealing in ways he didn't intend.

Each species has a unique talent tree of 26 nodes, distinct combat style, and their own 5-quest story arc that weaves through the main narrative.

AN ENTIRE WIZARD'S LIBRARY
Spells are unlocked by reading books. Not abstract skill trees — actual in-world books, with his margin notes, his anxieties, and the occasional deeply ill-advised experiment log.

The books tell you what he was trying to do. They also tell you what went wrong. Repeatedly.

CLASSIC ACTION-RPG COMBAT
Real-time isometric combat in the style of A Link to the Past: melee arc attacks, aimed spell projectiles, dodge rolls with i-frames, enemy tells you can learn to read.

Tower floors have designed encounter rooms — patrol rooms, ambush rooms, elite rooms, boss rooms — that reward spatial awareness over button mashing.

RECRUIT YOUR ARMY
Defeat enemies at low HP, spare them, and they follow you. Slimes, skeletons, imps, golems — they all have opinions about working for you and most of them are positive.

A WORLD WORTH EXPLORING
The overworld is procedurally generated: bog, forest, highlands. Settlements to discover. Dungeons to clear. A grumpy baron next door who has extremely valid grievances.

And somewhere out there: Arcanist Solmor, who has just returned home to find things significantly changed.

EARLY ACCESS TRANSPARENCY
We're a small team shipping a game we love. Early Access funds the full quest content, voice acting, and console ports. The core loop — combat, exploration, tower progression — is complete and polished. Content volume grows with every update.

Expected 1.0 timeline: 12 months.
```
