# Character Design — The Princess
> *Tomes, Towers & Transmutation*
>
> She had one window, one bookshelf, and no supervision.
> That was their first mistake.

---

## Core Identity

The player character is always **The Princess** — a young woman imprisoned by an
absent-minded wizard who simply forgot she was there. Her species, class, and
appearance are chosen at the start of each run, but her voice, her dryness, her
methodical approach to arcane revenge, remain constant across all forms.

Every species plays differently but shares the same narrative: she was locked in,
she taught herself magic from his own books, and she is leaving with interest.

---

## Biped Species (Current)

These are the twelve playable humanoid forms. Each has a distinct silhouette,
colour palette, and visual vocabulary for outfits and accessories.

---

### 🧑 Human

**Identity**: The baseline. Unremarkable in exactly the way that makes everyone
underestimate her. She compensates with competence.

**Body**: Medium height, balanced proportions, warm skin tones (cream through
deep brown). No inherent magical glow.

**Hair**:
- Short bob, practical ponytail, loose shoulder-length, or severe bun
- Colours: black, brown, auburn, blonde, grey-streaked (for the older variant)
- Occasional single streak of violet or silver (self-applied, from the wizard's
  dye experiments in cabinet three)

**Outfits**:
- *Scholar variant*: simple grey shift dress with ink stains, worn-out boots,
  canvas bag repurposed from the wizard's shopping list
- *Mage variant*: cobalt robe (stolen from the wardrobe on floor 4), brass-
  clasped cloak
- *Warrior variant*: practical leather vest over linen, short skirt-over-
  trousers, sensible boots that have clearly seen worse

**Accessories**:
- Wire-rimmed reading glasses (pushed up on head)
- A notebook (always)
- One borrowed quill she never gave back
- Candle stub in pocket

**What sets her apart**: She looks like she belongs in a library. She does not
look dangerous. This is accurate while also being misleading.

---

### 🧝 Elf

**Identity**: Slender, unhurried, slightly tired of everyone around her. Has been
alive long enough to find most things mildly amusing.

**Body**: Taller than human average, limbs slightly longer, neck graceful. Skin
tends toward cool undertones — pearl, silver-grey, soft olive, or pale gold.
Pointed ears, always.

**Hair**:
- Long and straight, often partially pinned back with something improvised
  (a letter opener, a piece of wire, the pen from the wizard's desk)
- Silver, moonlight white, very pale gold, or deep forest green (natural, not
  dyed — elves have opinions about dyeing)
- Loose waves are considered casual; elaborate braids indicate ceremony or
  extreme boredom

**Outfits**:
- *Scholar*: long layered dress in muted earth tones (sage, smoke, deep teal),
  draping sleeves, embroidered hem she definitely did herself while waiting
- *Mage*: asymmetric robe, one sleeve longer than the other for reasons she
  hasn't explained, silver clasp at the collar
- *Wanderer*: light cape over fitted tunic, split skirt for movement

**Accessories**:
- Thin tiara of braided silver wire (not a crown — she would like to make this
  distinction very clear)
- Single earring that is almost certainly a ward, not decoration
- Small knife worn at the thigh. Not threatening. Utility.

**What sets her apart**: The ears, the height, the faint luminescence in low
light. Also the expression that reads as "I have seen this exact situation before
and I know exactly how it ends."

### ⭐ High Elf

**Identity**: Ancient bloodline. She carries herself like she owns the tower. She
does not, technically, own the tower. She considers this a temporary situation.

**Body**: The most elongated of all forms — very tall, long-limbed, neck held
high. Skin has a faint inner glow, ranging from warm amber to cool blue-white.
Ears noticeably longer and more tapered than standard elf.

**Hair**:
- Always long. Always. To cut it would be to announce something.
- Platinum white, silver-gold, or deep black with a luminous sheen
- Complex updo with multiple sections, or entirely loose and trailing — never
  anything in between
- Small glowing motes occasionally drift from it in dim light. This is natural.

**Outfits**:
- *Formal*: sweeping layered gown, deep indigo or ivory, subtle geometric embroidery that wasn't sewn — it grew
- *Working*: structured robe with high collar, sleeves rolled up because there
  is work to do, thank you
- *Battle*: long split-skirt in midnight blue, fine chain at the waist, boots
  that go to the knee and have never once made noise

**Accessories**:
- Actual crown. Small, elegant, hers by birthright and she will not be discussing it
- Signet ring that does something (she has declined to specify what)
- A cloak clasp in the shape of a star — the kind of star that hasn't been
  visible from this hemisphere for four centuries

**What sets her apart**: The glow. The height. The posture. The faint impression
that she was already inconvenienced before she got here and has simply added this
to the list.

### 🧚 Pixie

**Identity**: Extremely small. Extremely fast. Not to be confused with "not a
threat." Her wings hum faintly when she is annoyed, which is most of the time.

**Body**: Under half human height, head proportionally larger, limbs quick and
slightly oversized for her frame. Pointed ears. Wings: semi-transparent, vaguely
iridescent, extend from upper-back. They do not fold neatly. She has accepted this.

**Hair**:
- Wild and short, usually, because long hair catches in the wings
- Lilac, rose, aqua, or sunset gold — naturally vivid
- Often accessorised with tiny objects she has collected (a bead, a button, a
  tooth from something she defeated that she would like to discuss)

**Outfits**:
- *Default*: dress made of something that moves — gauze, soft cotton, anything
  that doesn't weigh her down; usually in complementary-to-wings colours
- *Armoured*: tiny fitted plate over coloured underlayer, impractical and
  extremely correct about itself
- *Casual*: wrapped top and gathered skirt, bare feet because shoes are, frankly,
  a choice she resents

**Accessories**:
- Flower tucked behind ear (usually real; she has opinions about which ones)
- Something shiny on a thread around her neck
- Very small satchel, extremely overpacked

**What sets her apart**: The wings. The size. The fact that she can fit places
the others can't. The look on her face that says she has already thought of three
ways to defeat you and is selecting the funniest.

---

### 🌿 Fae

**Identity**: Not malicious — mercurial. The forest operates on a different set
of rules. She brought those rules with her.

**Body**: Between pixie and human in height, willowy, slightly weightless in
movement. Soft glow from the skin — cool green or gold. Wings similar to pixie
but larger, more leaflike, and occasionally slightly translucent. Pointed ears.

**Hair**:
- Long and textured, interwoven with small leaves, flowers, or fine vines
  (natural, not arranged — it simply grows that way)
- Forest green, autumn gold, deep umber, or pale silver
- Moves slightly in still air. This is normal.

**Outfits**:
- *Forest*: layered dress with irregular hem that looks like overlapping leaves;
  colours shift with the season she came from
- *Court*: floor-length wrap in iridescent silk that catches light differently
  depending on angle
- *Wanderer*: short dress or wrapped top and long skirt, split for movement,
  bare feet or soft leather

**Accessories**:
- Living vine braided into hair or worn at the wrist
- Ring of woven grass that is extremely powerful
- Small bag that contains more than should fit

**What sets her apart**: The green-gold aura. The way the forest responds to her
— plants move slightly toward her in every room. The wings when she uses magic,
which she only half-controls.

---

### 💀 Undead

**Identity**: Returned. Not quite dead, not quite alive, not particularly
interested in your opinion on the matter. Has been here before. It was different
last time.

**Body**: Pale to grey-blue to bone-white skin, slightly luminous in darkness.
Standard human proportions but with a sense of stillness, like she's not quite
breathing. Hair continues to grow. This is considered a positive sign by those
who think about it.

**Hair**:
- Greyscale: white, pale silver, or ashen grey with possible dark roots
  showing through (the last thing she remembers before is often her hair colour)
- Long, somewhat dishevelled, or arranged with deliberate care as if to prove a
  point
- Possible faint mist rising from it in warm air

**Outfits**:
- *Haunting*: long dark gown, slightly ragged at the hem, colour leached to near-
  black
- *Reclaimed*: neat dress in deep jewel tones (she refuses to wear grey; she is
  making decisions about what kind of undead she is)
- *Practical*: structured jacket over simple dress, boots, expression that dares
  anyone to comment on the skin colour

**Accessories**:
- Aura: faint cold light, pale blue or violet, always present
- Flower that hasn't wilted (she waters it; it's unclear how this works)
- Old ring on a chain that she looks at sometimes

**What sets her apart**: The skin. The aura. The faint smell of old books and
something that is not quite rain. The fact that she has seen several of these
towers before.

---

### 🐉 Draconic

**Identity**: Dragon-blooded. Proud of it. Not going to apologise for the scales.

**Body**: Strong build, slightly broader shoulders, visible scales along the neck
and forearms that catch the light. Skin tones: deep rust, bronze, charcoal, or
dark hunter green. Eyes are distinctly not mammalian — slitted iris, vivid colour.
No tail (or a very small one she can choose to have or not). No wings unless she
earns them.

**Hair**:
- Dark and strong, rarely moves unless fire is nearby
- Deep black, dark red, or the particular dark orange that is almost brown
- Short and blunt or long and braided — nothing that would tangle in anything
- Occasionally a single very straight lock that is somehow still on fire. She has
  learned to work around this.

**Outfits**:
- *Battle*: fitted armour over dark underlayer; the armour is partially scale
  (not hers — she harvested it)
- *Scholar*: structured robe in deep jewel tones, high collar, sleeves she can
  push back
- *Casual*: wrapped dress in terracotta or deep teal, cinched belt, practical
  boots

**Accessories**:
- Small horns (she doesn't hide them)
- Bracers that are partially decorative, mostly functional
- A small flame she can hold in her palm that she uses for reading

**What sets her apart**: The scales. The eyes. The fact that the library stopped
burning when she arrived, and she has very deliberately not explained this.

---

### ✨ Celestial

**Identity**: Descended from starlight, which she finds a slightly overwrought
way to put it. She prefers "from the sky originally, currently in a tower, working
on it."

**Body**: Tall, graceful, warm inner glow — gold or soft white or pale dawn-blue.
Skin occasionally shifts to translucent at the edges in very dark rooms. Pointed
ears, less sharply than elves. Hair moves independently of wind.

**Hair**:
- White-gold, silver-rose, or deep midnight blue with visible stars in it (natural)
- Long, always, and waving gently
- Does not need to be washed in the conventional sense

**Outfits**:
- *Divine*: layered gown in deep blue and gold, stars embroidered on the hem
  that are actually stars — i.e., they move
- *Practical*: long wrap dress in white or cream with gold trim, sleeves she can
  tie back
- *Working*: fitted robe, shorter, built for movement, the glow visible at the
  collar and cuffs

**Accessories**:
- Crown of pure light (she can turn it off; she usually leaves it on)
- Floating motes — small lights that orbit her when she's concentrating
- Aura: warm gold, always present, slightly irritating to those who find light
  irritating

**What sets her apart**: The glow. The hair. The sense that she is slightly more
real than the room she's standing in.

---

### 🍄 Gnome (Additional detail)

**Identity**: Short, methodical, extremely good at making things out of other
things. Arrived with nothing, currently has a functional shortwave communicator
and her own filing system.

**Body**: Under three-quarters human height, head proportionally large, hands
capable and quick. Rounder proportions than goblin, friendlier expression by
default.

**Hair**:
- Big and voluminous — gnome hair is a structural achievement
- White, silver, pastel yellow or lavender — often very light tones against dark
  clothing
- Up in a wild bun held by whatever implements are nearby, or loose and simply
  enormous

**Outfits**: Practical layering — apron over dress over underdress, pockets
everywhere, a hat she makes look intentional.

---

### 👺 Goblin (Additional detail)

**Identity**: Small, fast, and has already assessed every exit in the room.
Underestimated by everyone, including herself, which is a tactical advantage.

**Body**: Under four-fifths human height, large head, wide ears, angular features.
Skin: swamp green, bark brown, mossy grey, or sickly yellow. Large yellow-green
or amber eyes.

**Hair**: Sparse and wild, or extremely overdone (she has strong opinions about
appearance and they are inconsistent). Dark brown, black, or occasionally an
aggressive red.

**Outfits**: Asymmetric and assembled — layers of mismatched things that somehow
work, cinched with a belt she made, boots that are technically two different boots.

## Non-Biped Forms (Current)

### 🟢 Slime

**Identity**: An amoeba who taught herself magic by absorbing a spellbook. She
retains information differently. She is not sure if this is better or worse.

**Body**: Entirely amorphous, translucent to opaque, primary colour fully
customisable. No fixed features except two expressive eyes. Mimics shapes she
has seen but doesn't maintain them under stress.

**"Hair"**: N/A. She can form protrusions that serve a similar aesthetic function
if she chooses.

**"Outfit"**: Whatever she decides she looks like today. She can approximate a
dress.

**Accessories**: She can hold things. They become part of her temporarily. This
is fine.

**What sets her apart**: She moves differently. She fits through gaps. She is not
concerned about her appearance in the same way the biped forms are, but she has
developed opinions.

## Planned New Species

---

### 🦊 Foxling (Kitsune)

**Status**: Partial — the Fox model exists in the character manifest; needs full
DNA integration as a biped subrace.

**Identity**: Clever, quick, and has several more plans running simultaneously
than she mentions. Tail(s) indicate power level, though she usually keeps this
information to herself.

**Body**: Human-shaped but with fox ears and tail(s) — one by default, more as
power increases. Warm orange to deep fox-red skin, or silver-white for the rare
pale variant. Amber or green eyes with slightly larger iris. Light build, quick.

**Hair**:
- Red, orange, black, or striking silver-white
- Usually kept back — practical given the ears
- Braided or in a low tail; loose hair tangles with the fox ears and she has
  strong opinions about this

**Outfits**:
- *Mischief*: layered dress with asymmetric hem, warm autumn tones
- *Court*: formal kimono-adjacent layered robe in rust and gold
- *Battle*: short fitted jacket over long split skirt, room to move

**Accessories**:
- Tail charms (small bells or beads tied into the tail fur)
- Folding fan (functional for both cooling and intimidation)
- Nothing that jingles when she needs to be quiet (the fan has a silent mode)

**Subtypes**: One-tail (novice), Three-tail (adept), Nine-tail (mythic) — progression based on story milestones.

---

### 🌊 Naiad (Water Nymph)

**Status**: Planned.

**Identity**: From still water originally. Adapts. The tower is technically quite
damp if you know where to look, and she does.

**Body**: Fluid movement, slightly iridescent skin — the blue-green of deep water
or pale river-stone grey. Hair perpetually slightly damp. Small fins at the
temple or forearms (optional). Tall, willowy, rarely still.

**Hair**: Deep teal, sea green, or near-black with blue highlights. Long and
always partially wet. Does not dry.

**Outfits**: Flowing fabrics in water colours — she cannot tolerate anything
constricting. Long layered dress or wrapped sarong-adjacent garment.

**Accessories**: Pearls (she makes them; don't ask). Small vial of water she
carries. One coral ring.

---

### 🌙 Moonborn

**Status**: Planned.

**Identity**: Connected to lunar cycles in ways she considers a private matter.
She is stronger at night and has stopped pretending otherwise.

**Body**: Very pale skin with faint silver pattern lines — visible in moonlight,
faint in daylight. Dark eyes with faint silver ring around the iris. Tall, still,
powerful.

**Hair**: Silver-white from birth. Long, straight, lit from within at night.

**Outfits**: Deep blue-black to midnight purple. Long gown with gathered skirt, or
long fitted dress with cape. The silver pattern on the skin continues onto the
fabric.

**Accessories**: Crescent moon in the hair — natural growth, not jewellery. Silver
earrings. A cloak that pools on the ground and she has never tripped on, somehow.

**Subtypes**: Crescent (waxing power), Full (peak), Eclipse (something else entirely).

---

### 🌺 Verdant (Nature Princess)

**Status**: Planned.

**Identity**: The forest did not put her in the tower. The forest helped her find
the door. She is here on purpose.

**Body**: Warm brown to deep green skin tones. Small leaves or flowers occasionally
growing from the hair — not placed there, simply there. Vine-like pattern on the
arms. Eyes: vivid green or amber.

**Hair**: Deep forest green, dark brown, or autumn red. Thick, textured, with
growing things in it.

**Outfits**: Layered organic fabrics that move like leaves — greens, browns, living
colours. Long gathered skirt. Bare feet or soft bark-soled shoes.

**Accessories**: Living wreath. Seeds in a small pouch. A staff made from a
branch that definitely came from somewhere specific.

---

### 🔥 Ignis (Fire-Born)

**Status**: Planned.

**Identity**: Elemental origin. The wizard's fireplace tried to explain this to
him fourteen months ago. He filed it under "administrative anomalies."

**Body**: Deep amber to charcoal skin with faint ember-glow patterns. Hair is
literally flames — controlled, shaped, but fire. Eyes: vivid amber, orange, or
white-hot. Warm to the touch. Not hot enough to damage; hot enough to make a
point.

**Hair**: Flame. Shaped fire. Colours: orange and gold (standard), deep red
(intense), blue-white (something has happened and she will explain later).

**Outfits**: Fire-resistant by necessity. Layered in dark fabrics that show ember
patterns. Structured rather than flowing, because flowing and flame is a
complicated combination.

**Accessories**: None that are flammable. Obsidian and gold. The ember-glow
patterns are their own decoration.

---

### 👻 Specter

**Status**: Planned.

**Identity**: Not undead — different. She was always partially elsewhere. The
tower just made it visible.

**Body**: Semi-translucent. Can appear almost solid or almost entirely absent
depending on her mood and effort. Cool grey-blue. Hair and clothing trail
slightly behind her movement.

**Hair**: White or silver-blue, fades into mist at the ends.

**Outfits**: Whatever she was wearing, ghosted into a permanent state. Usually a
long dress in soft grey or pale blue, trailing.

**Accessories**: Things she can't quite put down — a book, a ribbon, an old key —
they stay with her.

---

### 🐍 Lamia (Serpent-Biped)

**Status**: Planned.

**Identity**: From the serpent archetype but adapted to biped reasoning. She
stands upright because she decided to. It took three weeks. She would do it
again.

**Body**: Human upper body, serpentine lower half (or legs with visible scale
pattern, depending on interpretation). Scales on the arms, neck, around the eyes.
Vivid scale colours: teal-gold, scarlet-black, emerald.

**Hair**: Dark, straight, often in a high coil or long and straight. Occasionally
replaced with very fine scale-ridges along the crown.

**Outfits**: Long wraps and layered fabrics that work with the silhouette. Fitted
upper garment, flowing lower.

**Accessories**: Tail decorations (rings, wrapped cord). Scale-pattern jewellery.

## Design Principles

1. **Every form reads clearly at small size.** Silhouette distinguishes species
   before colour or detail. Elf ears from 20 feet away. Slime is unmistakable.

2. **The princess is always the princess.** Different species, same competence.
   Visual design should suggest intelligence, agency, and a specific kind of
   focused dryness regardless of the form.

3. **Colour communicates story.** Celestial glows warm. Undead is cool and
   desaturated. Fae is too green. These are consistent and read in gameplay.

4. **Accessories are earned or found.** The crown isn't decorative — it's a
   statement. The Celestial's halo isn't jewellery. Things she wears have
   stories.

5. **No helpless princess silhouette.** Every dress, every skirt, every flowing
   robe should imply she can move in it. She has places to be.
